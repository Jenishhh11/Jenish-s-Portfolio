Effect of CRMB (Crumb Rubber Modified Bitumen) for Flexible Pavements.

- Crumb rubber used in CRM is derived from recycled tires.Recycled tires are processed to remove metal and other contaminants.
- Crumb rubber is blended with asphalt binder to create modified binder.Addition of crumb rubber to asphalt binder improves performance of flexible pavements, especially in high traffic and heavy axle load areas.
- Improved durability reduces need for maintenance and repairs, leading to cost savings for pavement owners and users.
- Use of recycled tire rubber in pavement construction diverts waste tires from landfills, reducing amount of waste that must be disposed of.
- Use of CRM reduces demand for virgin asphalt binder, which is a non-renewable resource.


Earthquake Resistant structure using Rocking Shear wall

- Main goal: to observe building behavior under lateral loads (earthquake) and minimize structural damage.
- Simulated building in Etabs and applied all loads on structure.
- Analyzed structure in various earthquake zones by adding respective earthquake parameters.
- Analyzed structure again with shear wall and compared inter-storey drift, lateral loads on each storey, and displacement of stories.
- Considered effect of infill masonry wall on building performance during earthquake period.
- Identified mathematical model to calculate infill wall stiffness.
- Modeled equivalent structure with same stiffness in Etabs.
- Compared the results with the addition of tendons, links and struts replacing the shear wall for real life assumptions.


High performance building envelopes

- Project consists of two parts focused on high-performance facades.
- First part identifies design methods for high-performance facades, differentiated by climate types.
- Characteristics of sustainable facades are discussed, including building orientation, window-to-wall ratio, shading elements, and daylighting methods.
- Material selection and their properties are discussed due to their influence on sustainable facade performance.
- Second part discusses building performance analysis steps, including energy modeling, daylight modeling, thermal comfort modeling, and heattransfer analysis.
- Strategies for incorporating analysis procedures with the design are presented, such as integration of different software tools and compatibility with BIM design authoring tools.
- Applications for high-performance facade designs are presented.
- Two case studies are presented to show implementation of strategies on architectural projects.
